Part 1: Airbnb Loyalty


The Problem
In our Service Design class at Northwestern San Francisco, My teammates Keren, Michael, and I were given the task of developing a paid membership program to alleviate pain points in the Airbnb guest journey and increase customer loyalty.

The primary user we decided to focus on serving is high-end leisure travelers.

By solving the guest's problem, we hoped to solve Airbnb's business problem.


Our Research
 Our first step in research was mapping out the guest journey, identifying pain points along the way. After this exercise we were most interested in the transportation and the stay portions of the guest journey. Airbnb does not currently have much to offer guests in the offline portion of their stay.



We then mapped out different stakeholders that influence guests when deciding to book an Airbnb. Through creating this map, we decided to look into connecting Airbnb to the web of travel loyalty that already exists.



The Concept
We came up with the concept for a paid membership program which would include a system of points and rewards earned after each booking. Points can be redeemed for a free night of stay after users hit 200 points. Airbnb members would also be eligible for assistance with transportation throughout their trip through a partnership with Lyft.



Part 2: Airbnb Concierge

In the second quarter working with Airbnb, we decided to do a deep dive into Airbnb's opportunity to give their users an elevated customer service experience.

Understanding Guest Needs
Still looking at high-end leisure travelers, we wanted to see what do HLTs want in a paid membership program? We found that they value priotized customer service.



Exploring Concierge Services
After discovering that HLTs value prioritized customer service and are interested in a concierge service, we decided to do a deep dive into how traditional concierges help their guests.



What do HLTs value in a concierge service?
We then asked HLTs what they value in a concierge service. We conducted a survey with 65 high-end leisure travelers and interviewed two of our users in depth.
We found that HLTs value security, responsiveness, and exlusivity







Prototyping a Text Concierge
We finished the project by prototyping a text concierge, focusing on the exclusivity value. We wanted to understand how to ingrain exclusivity into those experiences through a concierge service in order to appeal to the needs of HLTs.
