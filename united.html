designing a marketing campaign to get 18-22 year old travelers flying United

Project Overview
During my Sales internship at United, I took on an adjunct project in the marketing department back to Northwestern to create a promotional strategy for United's Young Adult discount. This project was covered this summer by The Washington Post.

Timeline: March - June 2019

Skills: Market Research - Focus Groups + User Interviews, Graphic Design

research /1
why are Gen Z travelers not flying United?

After surveying 250 students across college campuses, conducting focus groups at Northwestern, and comparing with competitors, we discovered the following insights:

1. Underclassmen typically do not book their own flights
2. Gen Z prioritizes the ethical and environmental impact of companies they purchase from
3. 18-22 year olds are mostly unaware of United’s MileagePlus program

recommendations /2
18-22 year olds are largely unaware of MileagePlus and feel disconnected with United’s brand values

After the research phase, we put together a deck of recommendations and presented to United's Marketing leadership based on the following insights:

Advertise and Engage on Social Media

1. Educate Gen Z on how to book through the app and use the discount - 60% of Northwestern students have never booked their own flight.

2. Use popular instagram and YouTube Gen Z travel influencers to advertise the discount and sponsor their trips. Influencers can educate viewers on how to properly use the discount in their videos.

Key Insight: Gen Z uses social media primarily for education and entertainment.

Resonate with Gen Z Value

1. Social Activism: Give students the option to use their discount to go towards a non-profit organization.

2. Evironmental Impact: Teach young adults how they can minimize their carbon footprint while flying, offset carbon emissions for those who use the discount.



Key Insight: 40% of Gen Z say they'd pay more for a product if the company promoted their beliefs

Improve Campus Engagement

Strengthen Campus Ambassador Program

1. Create consistent social media personas for each ambassador including hashtag usage and required weekly posting.

2. Instead of just one ambassador per campus, have multiple or rotate to avoid burnout and to reach different age groups with different travel habits.

Key Insight: United competitors, such as Delta, have had success in creating a strong campus presence.

results /3
United's MileagePlus Young Adult discount connects with Gen Z values and is featured on platforms they watch

United used our idea of sponsoring Instagram and Youtube travel influencers to go on trips and explain how to use the United Young Adult Discount. Influencers made Instagram stories explaining the program's conditions in depth as well as short videos embedded into their travel vlogs.

In addition, United decided to purchase carbon offsets for each young adult using the discount.

recognition /4
The Washington Post celebrated United's Young Adult program for being the first traditional airline to successfully market to younger customers
