United Airlines

Marketing Campaign Design

Project Overview During my Sales internship at United, I took on an adjunct project in the marketing department back to Northwestern to create a promotional strategy for United's Young Adult discount.

This project was covered this summer by The Washington Post.

Timeline March - June 2019

United Airlines

Developed marketing promotional strategy for United's Young Adult discount program.

Research
How to drive preference for 18-22 year old travelers?

Our Research Plan:
1. Survey 18-22 year old Travel Preferences
2. Competitive Analysis
3. On-campus Focus Groups

After completing each step of our research plan, we presented to United's market research team and they selected insights to run their own testing on.

Key Research Insights: Underclassmen do not typically book their own flights, Students care about the environmental ethics of the companies they buy from, Discounts are an incentivizer for this age group.

18-22 year olds are not aware of MileagePlus and are not connecting with United’s brand.
Key Recommendations

After the research phase, we put together a deck of recommendations and presented to United's Marketing leadership.

Resonate with Gen Z Values

1. Social Activism: Give students the option to use their discount to go towards a non-profit organization.

2. Evironmental Impact: Teach young adults how they can minimize their carbon footprint while flying, offset carbon emissions for those who use the discount.

Key Insight: 40% of Gen Z say they'd pay more for a product if the company promoted their beliefs

Advertise and Engage on Social Media

1. Educate Gen Z on how to book throuhg the app and use the discount - 60% of Northwestern students have never booked their own flight.

2. Use travel influencers to advertise the discount and sponsor their trips.

3. Take Hemisphere's editorial section "Three Perfect Days" to Instagram Stories

Key Insight: Gen Z uses social media for education and entertainment.

Improve Campus Engagement

Strengthen Campus Ambassador Program

1. Create consistent social media personas for each ambassador including hashtag usage and required weekly posting.

2. Instead of just one ambassador per campus, have multiple or rotate to avoid burnout and to reach different age groups with different travel habits.

Key Insight: United competitors, such as Delta, have had success in creating a strong campus presence.

“It’s pretty genius in the sense of, ‘We’re going to give you a discount because we want to encourage you to see the world, which you say is important, and we’re going to offset the environmental side effects of the travel. The combination really is a powerful message.” - Washington Post



Results


The following recommendations were implemented by United's marketing team:

1. Carbon Offsets for travelers using the discount

2. Social Media Plan: included influencer strategy, Instagram stories explaining the discount, and updated aesthetically pleasing feed photos.
